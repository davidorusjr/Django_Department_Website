Philosophy

The general philosophy of the Computer Engineering is to produce graduates with hard, soft and research skills that are useful in analyzing, evaluating, designing, developing, manufacturing, procuring, marketing, managing and maintaining the computing, electronics, communication, information processing, and operating systems embedded in computer hardware and devices used by individuals, and private and public organizations.


History of The Computer Engineering Degree Programme

History of Computer Engineering Degree Programme The Department is an academic and administrative unit with the Head of Department being responsible for coordinating, guiding and supervising teaching and research in the Department. The Computer Engineering programme started in September 2009 (2009/2010 academic session). It was housed in the Department of Electrical/Electronics and Computer Engineering in the College of Engineering of the University up till August 2021 when the Department was unbundled. The Department of Computer Engineering was carved out and has been a stand-alone Department in the last two sessions thus far. The Department of Computer Engineering runs a five year undergraduate degree programme consisting of ten (10) semesters that lead to the award of Bachelor of Engineering (B.Eng.) in Computer Engineering. In the first two semesters, the students are engaged in the College of Natural Sciences, where they learn courses related to fundamentals of basic sciences and general mathematics. During the same duration, they also undertake courses in engineering graphics.

Subsequently, upon completion of the two semesters, successful students with Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) of at least 2.0 and not more than the stipulated carryover units proceed to the 200 Level where they are introduced to the general engineering courses. The 200 Level culminates into an eight-week Student Work Experience Programme (SWEP) while the last six semesters are for core Computer Engineering courses. The structure of Computer Engineering courses as taught in Bells University is such that the students are introduced to and engaged in the core curriculum set out by the Benchmark Academic Standard (BMAS) of both the National Universities Commission (NUC) and the Council for the Regulation of Engineering in Nigeria (COREN). Sometimes additional courses are added to the courses suggested by the BMAS in such a way as to facilitate a total Computer Engineering graduate that is up-to-date with the current global advancement drive. The Department graduated its first set of graduates in the 2013/2014 academic session. As at 2022/2023, the department has produced ten (10) sets of graduates.
